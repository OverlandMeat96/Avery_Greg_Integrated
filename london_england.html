LONDON, ENGLAND

Algonquin provincial park is a 7,653 km2 area of natural forests, lakes , rivers, bogs, and marshes and is located in south eastern Ontario. The park was established in 1893 and is declared the oldest provincial park within Canada. Its origin began as a logging area before 1893, but then was converted to a provincial park after that year due to increased number of recreational users. This provincial park is now used today as a recreational area as well as a conservation area that holds many research studies about its flora and fauna
